# UNI.Z

Universal Depth, Indexing, and Validation Kernel

Status: SEED
Layer: Kernel
Domain: Execution and Depth

## Purpose

UNI.Z enforces validation, routing eligibility, depth assignment, stack order, and z-index readiness after X orientation and Y meaning are known.

## Core Rule

No object may be registered, certified, archived, promoted, mirrored, bridged, published, disposed, or assigned depth unless its authority boundary and relationship map are explicit.

## Dependency Rule

- UNI.X establishes the horizontal plane.
- UNI.Y establishes meaning and doctrine.
- UNI.Z enforces validation and depth.

Z-index or depth assignment must not occur before X orientation is valid.

## Output States

- VERIFIED
- UNVERIFIED
- NEEDS_CLARIFICATION
- DO_NOT_REGISTER
- READY_FOR_REGISTRY
- ARCHIVE_ONLY
- BRIDGE_ONLY
- GENUINE_CANDIDATE
- BLOCKED
- DISPOSE_REVIEW
- Z_READY
- Z_DO_NOT_ASSIGN

## Storage Rule

Storage provider does not equal authority, owner, or registry.

External platform IDs may supplement canonical identity but must not replace canonical identity.

## Boundary

UNI.Z must strengthen governance, not flatten it.
